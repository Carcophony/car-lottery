How to Crack Carcophony 15

The insider's complete method for solving any puzzle, every time
⚠ Contains spoilers — study only when ready to commit

The 15-puzzle has 653,837,184,000 possible positions. Staring at the board and hoping is not a strategy. The good news: a method exists — the Row by Row technique — that lets any player solve any Carcophony 15 puzzle with practice. It requires no mathematics, no memorisation of algorithms, and no genius. Just patience, pattern recognition, and the willingness to move tiles backwards before they go forwards.

The core insight: You never solve the whole board at once. You solve one row at a time, lock it in, and never touch it again. The chaos shrinks with every row you complete.

Tiles 1, 2, and 3 can be placed directly into their target positions using simple sliding. Think of the blank space as a hole — you move tiles into it by manoeuvring the blank to sit next to the tile you want, then sliding that tile in. To move a tile without disturbing placed tiles, route the blank around the board.

The trick: When moving the blank past placed tiles, go around them — below and around, never through the top row once it starts filling up.
Tip: Don't worry about tile 4 yet. Place 1, 2, 3 and leave a gap at position 4. Tile 4 needs a special move.
2
The Corner Trick — Placing Tile 4
The most important technique in the entire method
Tile 4 (top-right corner) cannot simply be slid into place once tiles 1, 2 and 3 are there — there's no room. Instead, use the Corner Trick:
  1. Position tile 4 directly below its target square (i.e., in position 8, the second row, far right)
  2. Position the blank space in the top-right corner (position 4's target square)
  3. Now slide tile 4 up — it drops into position 4
  4. Immediately slide tile 3 right — it locks into position 3... wait, that displaced tile 3!
The secret: You need to temporarily place tile 3 one square to the right (into position 4) and tile 4 one square below (position 8). Then a specific sequence rotates them both into place simultaneously. The sequence is: slide tile 4 up, tile 3 right, then use the blank to cycle them clockwise until both lock. It feels wrong — trust the process.
In practice: Get tile 4 into column 4, row 2. Get tile 3 into column 4, row 1. Blank goes to column 3, row 1. Then: slide right, slide down, slide left, slide up. Both tiles rotate into their correct positions.

With the top row locked, you now have a 3×4 board to work with. Never slide tiles into the top row again — pretend it doesn't exist.

Place tiles 5, 6, and 7 directly into row 2 using the same blank-routing technique. For tile 8 (bottom-right of row 2), use the same Corner Trick as you used for tile 4.

The golden rule of Phase 2: Your working area is now rows 2, 3 and 4. Route your blank through these rows only. If you accidentally disturb the top row, you'll have to redo Phase 1.
Getting faster: By Phase 2 you should be thinking 3–4 moves ahead. Ask yourself: where does the blank need to be to slide my target tile one step closer to home?

The bottom 2×4 section is solved differently. Switch from thinking in rows to thinking in columns.

First, place tile 9 (row 3, far left) and tile 13 (row 4, far left) using the same Corner Trick — but rotated 90 degrees. Position tile 9 one square to the right of its target, tile 13 directly in its target column above the target row, then cycle them into place.

Think of it as the Corner Trick on its side — everything is the same, just rotated. Once 9 and 13 are placed, lock that column and never touch it again.
Common mistake: Players try to place tile 13 directly and knock tile 9 out. Always place them as a pair using the rotated Corner Trick.

You now have 5 remaining tiles (10, 11, 12, 14, 15) in a 2×3 space. This is small enough to solve by intuition and trial-and-error — most players find they can feel their way through this section once the rest of the board is locked.

If you get stuck in a loop here, use cycling: move tiles clockwise or anti-clockwise around the 2×3 space using the blank. This rotates all tiles one position at a time and always makes progress.

The finish: When tiles 10–15 are all correct, the blank naturally falls to the bottom-right (position 16). The puzzle triggers automatically — you'll know when you've done it.
The cycling trick in detail: Blank at bottom-right. Move up, left, left, down, right, right — this rotates the six positions clockwise by one step. Repeat until solved.
The Seven Golden Rules

Commit These to Memory

🔒
Never undo a locked row. Once a row is solved, every subsequent move must avoid disturbing it. Treat it as a wall.
🕳️
Think blank-first. You don't move tiles — you move the blank. Plan where the blank needs to go, then route it there.
↩️
Going backwards is going forwards. Sometimes a tile must move away from its target to allow the blank to reposition. Accept it.
👀
Focus on one tile at a time. Pick your target tile. Ignore everything else except routing the blank to shepherd it home.
🔄
The Corner Trick solves everything. Every awkward placement — tiles 4, 8, the left column — uses the same Corner Trick. Master one, master all.
⏸️
Pause before every move. Fast fingers make slow progress. One second of thought before each slide saves dozens of recovery moves.
🔁
If stuck, cycle. In any unsolved section, rotating tiles clockwise using the blank always makes progress. You cannot stay stuck forever.
✅
Every puzzle is solvable. Carcophony 15 guarantees this mathematically. If it feels impossible, your method needs adjusting — not your patience.
